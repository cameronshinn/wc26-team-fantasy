How the simulation works

Each data point on the chart represents 5,000 independent simulations of the remainder of the tournament from that moment in time, using real results where available and simulated results for unplayed games. Win probability is the fraction of those runs where each manager finishes with the highest point total.

Group stage

Each unplayed match is resolved by drawing goals independently from a Poisson distribution. The expected goals for each team are set by the Elo gap between them:

λA = 1.35 × ed / 400     λB = 1.35 × e−d / 400

where d = EloA − EloB. At parity (d = 0) both teams average 1.35 goals — about 2.7 total, realistic for tournament football. A 400-point gap gives roughly 3.7 vs 0.7. Goals are capped at 8. Draws occur naturally when both teams score the same number.

Teams are ranked within their group using FIFA's tiebreaker order: points → head-to-head points → head-to-head goal difference → head-to-head goals scored → overall goal difference → overall goals scored. The top 2 from each group plus the 8 best third-placed teams (32 of 48) advance to the Round of 32.

Knockout stage

No draws — extra time and penalties are folded into a single win probability using the standard Elo formula:

P(A wins) = 1 / (1 + 10−d / 400)

A 400-point gap gives the stronger team a 90.9% win probability. A 200-point gap gives 75.97%.

Elo ratings

Team Elo ratings are sourced from eloratings.net and updated automatically each day. The historical odds chart uses the Elo ratings that were current on each snapshot date, so early-tournament odds reflect what the model knew at that time rather than today's ratings. The three host nations — USA, Canada, and Mexico — receive a +100 point home advantage bonus applied on top of their base rating in every simulation run.

Elo noise

At the start of each simulation run, every team's Elo rating is perturbed by a random offset drawn from a normal distribution (σ = 100 points). This models within-tournament variance — injuries, form, luck — and prevents heavy favourites from advancing at unrealistically high certainty. ~68% of runs adjust a team by ≤100 points; ~95% by ≤200 points.

Golden Boot

The simulation doesn't track individual scorers — it operates at the team level. As a proxy, each team accumulates a simulated goals tally: the actual goals scored in all group-stage matches (Poisson-drawn) plus 3 bonus goals per knockout win. The bonus rewards teams that advance deeper, since their star players accumulate more scoring opportunities across extra matches. The team with the highest tally in a given simulation run is awarded the Golden Boot, and its owner receives the +5 points. If the real Golden Boot winner is already known, that result is used directly instead.

Scoring

The manager with the highest total in a given run wins that run.
